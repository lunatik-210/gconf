# GCONF Telegram news formats

Use one format per selected topic. Word counts apply to the clean public post,
including the source attribution and CTA.

## `flash` — 40–90 words

Use for an operationally useful availability, access, pricing, or rollout
change. State what changed, who can check it, the uncertainty if rollout is
partial, and one action. Do not inflate a small update into a market thesis.

## `release_explainer` — 150–260 words

Use for a discrete product or model release. Answer:

- what shipped and when;
- where and to whom it is available;
- what changed versus the prior mode;
- which GCONF-audience task is affected;
- what is publisher claim versus verified behavior;
- what remains unknown;
- what small test to run.

## `trend_translation` — 220–360 words

Use only when at least two independent publishers support a wider transition.
Lead with the transition, not a list of releases. Show the shared pattern,
connect it to one behavior, name a counter-signal or limit, and propose a test.

## `gconf_field_note` — 150–300 words

Use only with verifiable first-hand team or GCONF evidence and publication
basis. Start from what was tried or observed, show the process and failure edge,
then explain what the experience suggests. Do not relabel external or community
work as a team result.

## `story_lore` — 150–260 words

Use when an event sequence itself explains a technical, cultural, or security
shift. Keep chronology compact, distinguish verified fact from internet lore,
and end with the practical meaning rather than the anecdote.

## Shared composition

Move through:

1. event or shift;
2. concrete before/after change;
3. meaning for one audience behavior;
4. evidence or grounded example;
5. material limitation;
6. one action.

Use short social paragraphs, consistent `AI` or `ИИ`, zero to three meaningful
emoji, and one CTA. Avoid the canonical forbidden clichés, artificial urgency,
unsupported multipliers, and claims that an agent will do everything.
