# GCONF Instagram design system

## Stable brand grammar

- 4:5 portrait canvas, 1080×1350 final size. This is the only active format.
- Warm white or lightly tinted paper field.
- Fine technical grid or ruled structure used quietly.
- Heavy black grotesk headlines with strong line breaks.
- Small monospaced metadata: `[ gconf ]`, slide number, category, dates.
- One launch-specific highlighter color used for words, labels, counters, chips,
  rules, or a full-bleed statement slide.
- Dense evidence slides alternate with airy statement slides.
- Flat editorial composition; minimal shadows; no decorative 3D AI clichés.

## Historical palette seen in the feed

- Electric blue: previous Vibe Coding announcement.
- Acid lime: AI Weekend quote/CTA.
- Highlighter yellow with magenta: earlier Vibe Coding cohort.
- Hot pink: audience-request and case posts.
- Mint/green: earlier fourth-flow cover.
- Cyan and orange: minor category chips.

Do not mechanically sample these colors. Use the feed to find an adjacent
highlighter hue that remains recognizably GCONF and distinct from the previous
launch.

## Current run palette

For `Vibe Coding · AI Ops` use:

- muted coral: `#D96C50`;
- soft peach: `#F0D8CF`;
- warm paper: `#F7F4EE`;
- graphite: `#171513`;
- utility grey: `#77716C`;
- grid line: `#E1DCD4`.

Coral remains a signal, not a field: at most 10–15% on information slides and
25% on the cover or final conditions slide. Keep slide 6 on warm paper; never use coral full-bleed.
Never use the previous launch's electric blue. Historical references enter
generation only through grayscale structural derivatives.
Grayscale does not make old copy current: every readable legacy word, number,
attribution, geography, profession, cohort fact or claim remains forbidden
unless it is present in the current copy inventory.

## Dense typography and master-frame header

- Match the old slide's vertical geometry. Create one blank launch master-frame
  with the visible header, then edit that same frame independently for every
  slide. Do not chain generated slides.
- Header labels follow the blue reference exactly: `[ gconf ]`; `[ 02 ] за 3
  недели`; `[ 03 ] программа`; `[ 04 ] запросы`; `[ 05 ] результат`; `[ 06 ]
  старт · gconf`, with matching `XX / 06` on the right.
- Dense-slide headings occupy no more than 12–15% of height.
- Use 30–36 px body copy and 25–30 px origins at 1080 px final width.
- Keep 80–96 px outer margins. Reduce font and vertical gaps before copy.
- Use heavy grotesk for hierarchy and a neutral grotesk for body copy; avoid
  turning every subheading into condensed uppercase.
- Do not apply metadata after generation. Header text, grid and pagination must
  already exist in each generated image. Compare their geometry with the
  approved master and reject drift outside the configured tolerance.
- Distribute content through the full usable height. The last meaningful block
  ends at y=1160–1290; reduce type and gaps before removing copy.

## Slide rhythm

1. Cover: boldest title, one transition, three condition chips.
2. Outcomes: five numbered rows, high readability.
3. Program: two-column six-block editorial grid.
4. Requests: six current quote-like rows with light dividers; never inherit old
   professions, locations, or attributions without current evidence.
5. Cases: six compact cards under `С чем уходят участники GCONF`; individual
   origin labels stay only in the internal evidence audit.
6. Conditions: statement, four complete tags, and a compact conditions table on
   warm paper. A CTA block is optional and must be omitted when its destination
   is unknown or the approved copy explicitly excludes it.
