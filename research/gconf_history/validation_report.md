## Validation Report

### Overall Assessment: Ready to share

The report is ready for internal strategy and announcement research. The launch
dates, announcement message IDs, and calculated intervals were independently
recomputed from the two Telegram exports. The remaining limitations are stated
in the report and do not change the main product-evolution conclusion.

### Methodology Review

- The analysis answers the requested question: how GCONF announcements,
  audience pains, participant cases, technologies, and successive launches
  developed over time.
- Public-channel evidence is used for launch-specific claims.
- Closed-community evidence is treated as alumni evidence and is not assigned
  to a cohort unless the author or an official message states the cohort.
- Facts, chronological inferences, and future editorial implications are
  separated.

### Issues Found

1. **Low — incomplete carousel text.** Some program details exist only inside
   Telegram images and are absent from the JSON export. The extracted
   announcement corpus therefore preserves all available post text but cannot
   guarantee every slide-level detail.
2. **Low — reconstructed numbering.** GCONF 5, 6, and 8 are inferred from the
   sequence of confirmed cohorts. The main report labels these mappings as
   inferences.
3. **Low — case verification.** Participant outcomes are reports from Telegram
   posts, not independently audited business or medical results. The report
   treats them as observed content signals, not verified efficacy claims.

### Calculation Spot-Checks

- 15 launch rows: verified against source message IDs and announcement dates.
- Days between launch dates: independently recomputed; no discrepancies.
- 63-day cadence from November 2025 through May 2026: verified.
- 40-day gap from May 25 to the July 4 experiment and 23-day gap from that
  experiment to July 27: verified.

### Visualization Review

- The cadence comparison uses a zero-based bar chart with calendar days as the
  unit.
- Experimental AI weekend and the upcoming July 27 launch are excluded from
  the main cadence chart and remain visible in the audit table.
- The final HTML passed desktop and narrow-width browser verification at 1440
  and 390 pixels, including source-dialog interaction.

### Required Caveats for Stakeholders

- Treat the numbered mapping of GCONF 5, 6, and 8 as a strong reconstruction,
  not an official naming claim.
- Treat participant cases as evidence of audience language, use patterns, and
  perceived outcomes unless separate proof artifacts are available.
- Do not use medical, statistical, legal, or financial cases as performance
  claims without independent expert validation.
